Abstract

Real-time detection and segmentation of wildlife species, particularly cryptic ones like the Houbara Bustard, is essential for conservation efforts. This research introduces a two-stage deep learning framework optimized for mobile deployment, integrating YOLOv10 for high-accuracy object detection and MobileSAM for efficient segmentation. Unlike previous approaches, we introduce a **Threading Detection Model (TDM)**, which enables parallel execution of detection and segmentation tasks, reducing inference latency. Our approach improves wildlife monitoring efficiency by leveraging **multi-threaded execution** to optimize resource utilization, significantly enhancing real-time performance on mobile AI platforms. We evaluate our model on a **40,000-image dataset** curated specifically for Houbara detection, achieving **mAP50 of 0.9627**, **mAP75 of 0.7731**, and **mIoU of 0.7421**. YOLOv10 processes frames in **43.7ms**, while MobileSAM completes segmentation in **107.5ms**, ensuring near real-time inference. Our dataset addresses the **scarcity of high-quality conservation AI datasets**, filling a gap in automated wildlife monitoring. This work contributes to advancing **real-time conservation AI, ecological research, and autonomous species tracking**.
