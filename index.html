Abstract

Real-time animal detection and segmentation in natural environments play a crucial role in wildlife conservation, enabling non-invasive monitoring through remote camera streams. However, these tasks remain challenging due to computational constraints and the cryptic appearance of many species. To address these limitations, we propose a two-stage deep learning framework optimized for mobile platforms. Our approach integrates YOLOv10 for high-accuracy object detection and MobileSAM for efficient segmentation, ensuring both efficiency and accuracy. We introduce a Threading Detection Model (TDM) that enables parallel execution of detection and segmentation tasks, reducing inference latency while optimizing resource utilization. Our framework is evaluated on the Houbara Bustard, a priority species in conservation research. Results demonstrate high accuracy, achieving: - mAP50 of 0.9627, - mAP75 of 0.7731, - mIoU of 0.7421 YOLOv10 processes frames in 43.7ms, while MobileSAM completes segmentation in 107.5ms, ensuring real-time inference. We also introduce a 40,000-image dataset curated for Houbara detection and segmentation, filling a critical gap in AI-driven conservation. Beyond wildlife monitoring, our framework extends to real-time detection and segmentation on resource-constrained mobile platforms, benefiting ecological research, conservation robotics, and behavioral studies.
